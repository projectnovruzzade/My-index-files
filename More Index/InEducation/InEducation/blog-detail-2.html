Kamal Abdullazada

I graduated from Azerbaijan State Oil and Industry University and earned my Bachelor of Arts in Chemistry in 2016 from Denison University, where I also minored in Mathematics and Educational Studies. After my undergraduate work, I moved to Richmond, Indiana, to attend Earlham College, where I earned my Master of Arts in Teaching in. I completed my student teaching at Connersville High School as part of that program, after which I came to Tri for my first year of teaching. I believe students learn science by doing science, and that my classes are a medium through which my students will learn life skills that are critical for their success in college and future careers. As our students must be prepared to work cooperatively in a global and technological society where many of them will eventually hold jobs that do not yet exist, I emphasize critical thinking and problem solving, using our science content to develop good evidence-based reasoning and communication skills. In short, my teaching philosophy is: Diligent, motivated students who are responsible for their own learning will become autonomous thinkers, discerning moral agents, and engaged citizens.

I look forward to working with you and your students!
